Custom Tailored Inspections

Strycon inspections are custom-tailored to your facilities exact range and types of assets, we don't believe in one-size-fits-all checklists.

Material handling equipment comes with different manufacturer specifications. We don't ignore them to save time. During our initial asset survey, Strycon obtains the specific maintenance manuals for your exact equipment. Then, we stack those manufacturer points on top of standard OSHA, ANSI, and ASME regulations to create a bulletproof inspection form.

Our technicians execute a rigorous, two-tiered inspection process:

  • Frequent Checks: Visual and audible testing for accelerated wear, plus full functionality tests across the equipment's entire travel area.

  • Periodic Checks: Deep-dive inspections of electrical panels, precise measurements of brake thicknesses and air gaps, gear oil verification, a meticulous, inch-by-inch lifting medium review, and structural wear and tear.

Our approach isn't just about compliance; it’s about utilizing all data that can be taken from an assets condition. We catch the vulnerabilities less robust inspectors miss so you can plan repairs—providing you with proactive maintenance plans rather than reactive maintenance.

Clear Reports, Zero Guesswork

Strycon technicians are trained to use all five senses to hunt down issues. Whether we spot an OSHA violation, or a bolt feels loose, a motor sounds off, an electrical panel smells hot, or the oil tastes funny—it gets documented. Strycon doesn't skip the small stuff. We document everything.

Every one of our inspection points is broken down by a simple, four-color flagging system:

  • GREEN: All clear. No issues.

  • YELLOW: Heads up. Note on condition or recommended preventative maintenance.

  • ORANGE: Action required. Schedule a repair or address a minor OSHA violation.

  • RED: Shut it down. Imminent danger to your machine, facility, or crew, or a major OSHA violation.

You shouldn't need an engineering degree to understand the health of your own facility. Strycon’s reporting gives you an immediate blueprint of your compliance status, upcoming repairs, and productivity upgrades.

You don't have to just take our word for it, our technicians photograph every single violation, deficiency, and concern. You see exactly what we see, giving you total context, undeniable proof, and complete peace of mind that a real inspection actually happened.

No Second Trips, No Wasted Time

You've approved a repair - now what? With most companies, work grinds to a halt while a technician is sent back out just to pull part numbers. This costs extra fees and unnecessary downtime.

At Strycon, we plan for the repair before the breakdown even happens. During our initial asset survey, Strycon technicians photograph every nameplate on your equipment—including all motors, gearboxes, and control panel devices. This data is logged into our system on day one, so when a part is needed, we order it immediately. No second site visits required.
